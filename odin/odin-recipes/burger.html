Burger

Description

A classic burger is an all-time favorite for many, perfect for a quick meal or a weekend BBQ. With a juicy beef patty, fresh toppings, and a soft bun, itâ€™s the perfect balance of flavors and textures. You can easily customize your burger with different cheeses, sauces, and veggies to suit your taste. Whether you're grilling outdoors or making it on the stovetop, this burger recipe is simple and delicious!

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef (preferably 80% lean, 20% fat)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • 4 slices of cheese (American, cheddar, or your favorite type)
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Tomato slices
  • Onion slices
  • Pickles (optional)
  • Ketchup, mustard, and mayonnaise (or any other sauces you like)

Steps

  1. Preheat your grill or stovetop pan to medium-high heat.
  2. Divide the ground beef into four equal portions and shape each into a round patty about 3/4 inch thick. Season both sides of the patties with salt and pepper.
  3. Grill or cook the patties in the pan for about 4-5 minutes per side, or until they reach your desired level of doneness. If using cheese, place a slice on each patty during the last minute of cooking to melt.
  4. While the burgers cook, toast the hamburger buns on the grill or in a toaster until lightly golden.
  5. Once the patties are done, place each one on the bottom half of a bun.
  6. Top the patty with a slice of cheese, followed by lettuce, tomato, onion, and pickles (if using).
  7. Spread ketchup, mustard, and/or mayonnaise on the top bun, then place it on top of the burger.
  8. Serve immediately with your favorite side, like fries or a salad. Enjoy your homemade burger!
